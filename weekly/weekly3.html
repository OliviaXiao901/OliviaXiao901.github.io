Weekly Response 3

How is materiality manifested in poor images?

As a copy in motion, the poor image is "a ghost of an image, a preview, a thumbnail, an errant idea, an itinerant image distributed for free, squeezed through slow digital connections, compressed, reproduced, ripped, remixed, as well as copied and pasted into other channels of distribution". Though a high-resolution image looks more impressive and mimetic, the value of poor image can go far beyond the meaning of image itself.

The visuality of the poor image is resolutely compromised, which means that it is blurred and amateurish, so it actually create a space for viewers to participate in the creation of the image meaning. As the users actively re-edit and distribute the originally poor image, people can become editors, critics, translators and co-authors of poor images. Each new edition of the poor image reflects the personal thoughts of co-authors, which is a "snapshot of the affective condition of the crowd". When the image goes beyond the reality presenting through the image and illustrate a new level of reality which conveys the crowd neurosis, paranoia and fear, the materiality does not diminish with decrease of the resolution but manifests through the ambiguity.

In this way, the poor image, in fact, "constructs anonymous global networks just as it creates a shared history". Since people distribute the poor images after they do their reedition, the circulation of poor images create a "visual bonds". When people share their emotions and ideas through the poor image, the empathy between people gradually generates a new space to understand and present the materiality, which can expand to different perspectives. That is how the poor image not just reflect the materiality but manifest it.
